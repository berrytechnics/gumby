The Story

This truck rolled off the line in Flint, Michigan in 1970 — the last uninhibited year of American truck production before emissions regulations changed everything forever.

It was sold new at Hoffpauier Chevrolet in Lampasas, Texas, where the dealership installed a Ruffneck HD bumper — manufactured by Perry Co. in Waco — with the dealership's name cast permanently into the steel. That bumper is still on this truck today.

The configuration tells the story: long bed, factory air conditioning, heavy duty bumper, gun rack. This was a working ranch truck, ordered by a Hill Country rancher who needed a truck that could work as hard as he did.

It spent approximately forty years in Mississippi under a single long term owner — a non-salt state — which is largely why the frame is solid and the bones are strong after 55 years.

In 2026 it came home to Picayune, Mississippi, where it is being maintained and restored as a proud daily driver.
